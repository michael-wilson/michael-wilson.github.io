Special Issue on Root-Environment interactions in Physiologia Plantarum

Physiologia Plantarum has the tradition of publishing Special Issues, gathering reviews, original research articles and technical focus on a specific topic. They represent a snap-shot of the current research in a particular area. Being published in a Special Issue offers multiple benefits. First, it increases visibility, as a specific article is strengthened by all the other articles on the same theme published simultaneously (Special Issues have a higher citation rate). Second, it also increases the manuscripts’ accessibility as all articles have a free open access for 6 months (counting from the issue publication date).

Interested? Submit your manuscript via our ScholarOne Manuscripts site.

  • In Step 1, choose “Special Issue article” as type of article no matter what kind of articles you are submitting (original research, minireview, technical focus…)
  • In Step 5, choose one of the editors organizing the Special Issue. Your choice of editor is indicative but may speed up the editorial process.
  • In Step 6, specify in your cover letter that your article is meant to be part of a Special Issue and name which special issue “rooting2021” (we have often several in parallel).
